About the Course

This international-level short course provides advanced and practice-oriented insights into geotechnical earthquake engineering. With increasing global emphasis on resilient infrastructure, understanding soil–structure interaction and seismic hazard mitigation has become critical.

The course integrates engineering seismology, dynamic soil behavior, site response analysis, liquefaction assessment, and performance-based seismic design of foundations and retaining systems. Real case studies and hands-on exercises ensure direct industry applicability.

Why This Course Matters

  • Rapid urbanization has amplified seismic risk exposure.
  • Infrastructure resilience demands advanced geotechnical evaluation.
  • Site-specific effects significantly influence structural performance.
  • Liquefaction remains a major cause of earthquake-induced damage.
  • Modern design requires integration of hazard, response, and performance-based assessment.
